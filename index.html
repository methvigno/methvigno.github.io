Roles in robotics & autonomy

I work on robotics problems where simulation, decision-making, and software engineering meet messy reality: autonomous systems that have to recover from things their designers didn't anticipate, in environments that don't cooperate.

My recent thesis at NASA JPL was an exercise in exactly that. I built Gremlin, an adversarial framework that stress-tests autonomous decision-making in closed loop, surfacing failure modes before they reach hardware.

What drives me is building intelligent autonomous systems that are genuinely robust. I've worked across control, mapping, perception, simulation, and V&V on quadrupeds, drones, mobile robots, spacecraft, and launch vehicles, always caring about whether things work in practice, not just in theory.

Probabilistic Gas Mapping & Source Localization

EPFL · DISAL Lab · 2024 — 2025

Implemented Gaussian Markov Random Field models with Gaussian Belief Propagation for offline reconstruction of gas concentration fields from logged mobile-robot measurements, enabling gas-plume modeling and source localization.
